About

I come from a lineage of healing women

I'm Dr. Jasmine Therese Esguerra — a chiropractor, mother of three daughters, and a healer carrying forward the wisdom of the women who came before me. For over 23 years, I've blended modern chiropractic care with Hilot, the traditional Filipino healing art that runs in my blood.

My greatest lessons have been learning to honor our yin and feminine gifts — to forgive, to embrace the whole family, and to love unconditionally. As Mrs. Maui Filipina 2025, I carry a vision rooted in faith, Filipino heritage, and the belief that true wellness isn't static. It's alive, relational, and cyclical.

I founded Pure Wellness Love and The Well-Made Foundation because I believe when wellness multiplies, communities heal.

My Healing Work

Healing Art

Living LOVE

Healing comes from connection to our divinity... then life flows with ease and courage.

Our spirit desires to be fully embodied while our body desires to be inspired. Living LOVE is the healing art I've developed from understanding and working with our vibrational being. It's both a practice and a way of life — standing heart-forward, open, and free.

In this posture of victory and ease, healing becomes natural. Families become the true hubs of wellness — not through technique or correction, but through authenticity, truth, and the power of feeling seen and loved.

"Each season has its gifts. When we live in alignment with that light, our bodies and our world begin to heal."

Our logo carries two symbols: the Sampaguita — the Jasmine flower, national flower of the Philippines, meaning "sumpa kita," a promise of love. And the Sand Dollar — which, when broken open, releases five peace doves. Together they remind us: with the covering of these five pillars, we bloom from within. Flowers can't be forced to bloom... our approach is delicate, whole family, and honoring.

Our Cause

The Well-Made Foundation

I believe we are meant to live in harmony with nature's rhythms — honoring the seasons of expansion and rest, action and integration. The Well-Made Foundation is my commitment to restoring safety, dignity, and opportunity for families, and to helping end human trafficking through wisdom, love, and leadership.

My vision: one million members, each contributing $20 a year, to fund education, prevention, and holistic rehabilitation. When wellness multiplies, communities heal.
